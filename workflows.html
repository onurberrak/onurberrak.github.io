// 01 – outbound machine

From ICP filter to booked call, on autopilot.

Apollo finds the people, Claude scores them, Instantly runs the sequence, HubSpot tracks the deal. The SDR motion without the SDR.

Apollo.io Claude Instantly.ai HubSpot n8n Slack
ICP search saved in Apollotrigger

Daily cron pulls new contacts matching the ideal customer profile – title, company size, region, hiring signals.

Enrich + dedupeApollo · n8n

Pull full profile: role, tenure, recent activity, mutual connections. Drop anyone already in HubSpot as an active deal or opt-out.

AI fit + intent scoringClaude

Prompt evaluates: does this lead look like our best customers? Returns a 1–10 score with a one-line "why" used downstream.

score ≥ 7 ? branch
High-fit → personalised sequenceClaude → Instantly

Claude drafts a personalised first e-mail using the enrichment + score-reason. Instantly fires the 4-touch sequence at regional cadence.

Low-fit → nurture or archiveHubSpot

Drops into a long-cadence nurture list, or auto-archives with a "revisit in Q+2" reminder. No human attention burned.

Reply detectedInstantly → n8n

Webhook fires on any inbound reply. Claude classifies it: interested, not now, wrong person, unsubscribe.

Hot lead → KAM assignedHubSpot · Slack

Interested replies create a HubSpot deal, assign a KAM, ping Slack with the original reply and a one-line context summary.

// outcome

Replaces the SDR-tier of an outbound team. Sales reps wake up to a Slack list of warm replies with full context attached – not a queue of cold names to call.

// 02 – inbound lead triage

When the form fires, someone's already on it.

Website contact form → enriched, scored, routed in under 60 seconds. The rep gets a fully briefed lead, not a name and an email.

Webhook Apollo.io Claude HubSpot Slack n8n
Contact form submissiontrigger

Anyone hits "Get in touch" on the marketing site, n8n catches the webhook within seconds.

Enrich on the flyApollo

Pull title, seniority, company size, region, tech stack signals – everything the rep would manually google.

AI qualification + briefClaude

Reads the form's free-text message + enrichment, returns: lead score (1–10), three discovery questions tailored to them, two hypotheses about their problem.

priority routing
Hot (≥ 8) → KAM Slack pingSlack

DM to the on-duty KAM with the brief, the form text, the Apollo profile and the three suggested questions. Response target: 1 hour.

Warm (4–7) → nurture dripHubSpot

Adds to the "engaged but not ready" sequence. KAM reviews weekly digest.

HubSpot deal + activity logHubSpot

Every lead lands as a structured record: form text, AI brief, score, routing decision. Pipeline stays clean, no orphan e-mails.

// outcome

Inbound goes from "queue someone will check tomorrow" to briefed, routed and acted on in under an hour. The KAM walks into the call already knowing the customer.

// 03 – half-yearly review prep

The brief writes itself, 7 days before the meeting.

Every key account gets a strategic review every six months. The prep work – pulling data, drafting the agenda – is the part that used to eat the morning. Now it lands automatically.

Calendar trigger HubSpot Gmail / Outlook Claude Notion Slack
Review meeting in calendar · T-7 daystrigger

Cron finds upcoming review meetings tagged in the calendar, fires the prep workflow seven days out.

Pull 6-month account historyHubSpot · n8n

Revenue last 6 months vs. prior 6, every closed deal, every escalation, every open ticket, every product expansion conversation logged.

Pull e-mail thread digestGmail / Outlook

Last 6 months of e-mail history with the account – timestamps, sentiment markers, unresolved items, open commitments on both sides.

Generate one-page snapshotClaude

Claude assembles a structured page: revenue health · growth conversations · what they need from us · what's new on our side · three talk-tracks ordered by importance.

Drop in Notion + Slack reminderNotion · Slack

Snapshot lands in the account's Notion page, KAM gets a Slack DM with the link and a 60-second summary. Review prep done before the kettle boils.

// outcome

The cadence I built across my book turns from "find half a day to prep" into a pre-filled brief waiting in Notion. KAM shows up to the meeting having already digested six months of context.

// 04 – renewal radar

Renewals surfaced six months early.

Surprises at renewal mean someone wasn't paying attention. This loop watches the contract calendar so the KAM doesn't have to.

Daily cron HubSpot Claude Calendar Slack
Daily 06:00 scan of contractstrigger

n8n runs every morning, queries HubSpot for contracts expiring in T-180, T-120 and T-60 days.

Pull engagement signalsHubSpot

For each account in the window: last 90 days of e-mail activity, support tickets, NPS movement, recent escalations, expansion conversations.

AI risk-scoreClaude

Returns: at-risk / on-track / expansion-opportunity, with a one-paragraph "why" the KAM should care.

routing by risk
At-risk → Slack alert + actionSlack

Posts to the KAM channel with the account, the risk reason, a suggested intervention, and a calendar event for a recovery conversation.

On-track → renewal motion queuedCalendar · HubSpot

Pre-books the renewal conversation, drafts the renewal e-mail, opens a deal in HubSpot. KAM reviews and ships.

No renewal walks in coldoutcome

Every contract has a status six months out. Nothing surprises the team in renewal month, because everything was surfaced when it could still be moved.

// outcome

Turns "we lost X at renewal" into "we saw X drifting in Q-2 and pulled it back." The renewal calendar runs the rep, instead of the other way around.

// 05 – crisis escalation routing

Bad news caught before it festers.

Negative-sentiment e-mail lands. Within minutes it's classified, routed to the right team, and a draft acknowledgement is ready for the KAM to send. No e-mails buried under "I'll get back to you."

Inbox watcher Claude Slack HubSpot
Customer e-mail with negative sentimenttrigger

Inbox watcher flags messages with words like "delay", "complaint", "missing", "unacceptable", or AI-detected negative tone.

AI triageClaude

Classifies the issue: operations / billing / product / SLA. Severity 1–3. Identifies what the customer expected vs. what happened.

route + draft
Slack channel routingSlack

Posts to the right internal channel – ops, billing, product. KAM is always tagged so they own the customer-facing thread.

Acknowledgement draftClaude

Drafts a 24h-playbook-compliant reply: acknowledge, name the expectation + damage, hold off on root cause. KAM reviews and ships.

Customer hears back within 1 houroutcome

No e-mail goes unanswered for more than an hour. The KAM never has to draft from a blank page in crisis mode.

// outcome

The Sales OS §07 crisis playbook, automated. "Be early on bad news" stops being a rule and starts being the default behaviour of the inbox.
